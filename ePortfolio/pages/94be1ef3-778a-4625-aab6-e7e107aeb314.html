Who Is Robert D Rees?
 
 
 

I was born March 4, 1974 in Oakdale, California, in the heart of the San Joaquin valley.  I am second in a family of five, one older sister and three younger brothers.  From the beginning I have been spirited, a free spirit, mostly choosing the road less traveled.  I have always had talents for anything that is math, science, or technology based.  For most my life I have had a near obsession with computers and have tried to educate myself as technology has progressed.  First introduced to them in my adolescence then later reunited in my late teens.  Despite my love for technology life dictated otherwise, and I experienced an accomplished career in the construction industry.  However, my life could best be described by the significant milestones that have transpired through my adolescence, teens, twenty’s, and thirty’s.    These milestones do not describe me; however, they have defined the person I have become.

My adolescence was spent in California, mostly on an agricultural farm, with my grandparents.  We lived so close to them that I found myself on the farm more that at our house.     At this time you would either see me with my Grandfather or my Uncle tagging along in their daily duties on the farm.  I had learned many things from my time there, most of which I would not fully understand or even remember till later in life.  These men played a significant role on the man I would become.  In my late adolescence I was introduced to computers, and the obsession had begun.  Toward the end of my adolescence I had become unmanageable for my mother, and legal issues had begun.  Issues that I would not see an end to until my early thirties!

By my teens I found myself in Texas, reunited with my father.  I was the typical teen boy, rebellious and difficult at best, again choosing the road less traveled.  We lived in the country where there was no technology to be seen, and our school at the time did not incorporate computers until the end of my high school career.  I do however remember when they did incorporate them, as I helped my typing teacher to a small degree in my spare time set up the school’s first computer lab.  In high school I excelled in math and science, when I wasn’t being a teen, and found myself dabbling in sports when my grades allowed.  I was introduced to construction at this point in school and at home as I helped my father rebuild the family house.  Toward the end of my teens I found myself in the United States Army, mostly out of necessity, a short lived venture to say the least.  After the military, I started working in the construction industry, despite my love and desire for technology.

My twenties were a tumultuous time, as I tried to find myself.  I found myself in more legal issues, which estranged me from my family for my twenties and mostly through my thirties.  I had, by this time, been in construction for a few years, and did not know it at the time, but this would be the path I would follow until my mid-thirties.  My early twenties was a memorable time, with the realization of an illness that changed my life forever.  I flourished in the construction industry, and traveled, building structures along the way.  I grew to love construction, all the while keeping as up to date as I could with technology.  By my late twenties my legal issues had compounded and I found myself locked up in a state penitentiary.

My thirties was a time of rebirth, with my release from prison in my early thirties.  I once again went back to construction, and once again flourished in the industry.  I rose up through the ranks and reached what I had set out to do in construction, to be the boss.  I had decided that if I was going to do construction I would stick with it till I was.  I quickly reached that goal, and stayed with it for many more years.  Although I loved what I did, I still longed to work with technology.  So, in my spare time I built computers for people, friends, family, friends of friends, you name it.  But I still was not satisfied I loved to program, that’s what I wanted to do.  So I started my journey with college.  I went at night and weekends, and taught myself in my spare time.  In my mid-thirties my construction career came to an end when I was placed on disability for my illness.  After some time on disability I decided I had to do something, so I decided to finally achieve my deep desire for technology and go back to school to hopefully one day be in the technology industry.

I have had a full life to say the least.  My many experiences and choices may not have always been the right ones, but they have made me who I am.  As my Grandfather once said to me as a child, “If you can look at yourself in the mirror, and be proud at what you see, that is all that matters”  I may have taken the long way around, but I can honestly say that I am proud of what I see in the mirror.  I am proud of the man I have become.  I don’t think I would change anything if I could do it all over again, because my experiences have made me the man I am today.  I have many more years to go, and in those years I plan on realizing my dreams.  I know that when my time is done on this earth, I will look back with no regrets.
