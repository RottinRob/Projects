Exploration of Integrity

 

          As many would believe, there are about as many view point on integrity as there are books written about the subject.  According to Stephen L Carter “integrity requires three steps: 1 discerning what is right and what is wrong; 2 acting on what you have discerned, even at personal cost; 3 saying openly that you are acting on your understanding of right and wrong”. (pg. 7)  Dr. Barbara Killinger, a clinical psychologist, has psychological prospective on integrity “integrity is an internal state of being that guides us towards making morally wise choices”. (Intro.)  When I think of integrity I simply consider the persons actions in the decisions they make, how they interact and treat the people around them, and if they will stand by their values even when there is pressure from others.

Carter explores integrity as “perhaps the first among the virtues that make for good character is that it is in some sense prior to everything else: the rest of what we think matters little if we lack essential integrity”. (pg. 7)  Integrity guides every part of our world, it is how we live, how we think, how we act, and how we treat others, it is our compass in life.  I still believe that the easiest and most complete description of integrity comes from Dr. Killinger:

“Integrity is a personal choice, an uncompromising and predictably consistent commitment to honor moral, ethical, spiritual, and artistic values and principles”

          So, integrity is a choice, a commitment to one’s self to true to their beliefs, and the courage to stand by those values.  With the times as they are, it does seem difficult to find people with integrity.  So how do you know if a person has integrity or not?  Dr. Killinger describes integrity in action:

“As persons of integrity, we choose appropriate ways to express our anger…we choose to be reliable and predictable…we actively choose to remain loyal…we choose to listen carefully to oppositional views…we choose to be well informed…we choose to learn for our own mistakes” (pg. 14)

Make no mistake about it a person of integrity is not a perfect person, they know their values, they actively uphold them, and most importantly they learn from their own mistakes.  Carter bests put it:

“A person of integrity lurks somewhere inside each of us: a person we feel we can trust to do right, to play by the rules, to keep commitments” (pg. 7)

 

          So everybody has the potential to be a person of integrity, it’s a conscious choice that we make every day.  It is a trait that I feel people learn over time; some just learn it earlier than others.  Maybe it is a trait that could be taught, maybe then this world may start to get along.
